+1 For test driven development.

Our group is small, and we have the opportunity to influence the
methodology of how OpenViewer will be created for years to come.  We
should aim for a process that tries to keep a real view of the quality
of our application.  I feel test driven development is that way.
Break down our application into small testable chunks of code.

I think this is good in many ways.  For example, just yesterday,
Harrison was having a trouble testing OgreDotNet in isolation.  We did
not know what his issue was exactly,  So I created an nunit test so he
can run a ogredotnet 3d window alone without any other dependencies.
With such tests our our library, we can give people a suite of tests
that people can run on their respective operating system and runtime
to verify our code works under our assumptions, or to aid them in
resolving issues when they do not function.

- Daedius
