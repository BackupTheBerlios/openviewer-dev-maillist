Hi Daedius

Thanks for the feedback.  I am probably completely off base here (I 
usually get it wrong).  My logic was to take a "how would Steve Jobs 
create a 3D browser for an Apple" approach.    What  I was trying to 
convey was the concept of leveraging and working within the 3D immersive 
space versus an external or overlay 2D menu systems. 

Example: 
Your in Deepgrid on fantasy island  at coordinates 123.452.205 and you 
render a new door.  The door automatically configures itself for the 
appropriate information (Same as a landmark today but with additional 
server information and settings).  A copy of the door is stored in a local 
database on your PC as well as in world. 

Later that day meet someone in  OSGrid tell them about your place and 
render the door you made.  They walk through the door and are in OSgrid at 
your house. (Check out croquet) 

I fully agree we absolutely need to be able to configure and customize 
settings (click on door up pops an in world control panel and enter all 
the settings)  but hopefully that's rare as it's so much easier from the 
user perspective if they simply walk through a pre configured door. 

In terms of all the other configurations could this all be made just as 
intuitive using an in world 3D control panel with a bunch of prims on it.  
Click on the phone prim to configure voice, click on sun prim to configure 
sky click on a voice prim to chat, camera prim for camera etc etc.   This 
way even my Mother could use it and if she needed help she could render up 
her control panel give me access then watch as I show her how to do 
something. 

Anyway just food for thought.

Thanks for the link Nink,

I think if openviewer ever became an embedded plugin into a firefox
browser and was the focus of connecting into a single metaverse, you'd
be correct.   Honestly though, open viewer as it looks like now with
GTK UI is going to be used to connect into a variety of different
types of grids.  Each with specific settings and saved or not saved
passwords.  While using a URL as a shortcut into the whole process is
beneficial, a user still might need more configuration over how they
are connecting and how they interact with features the grid may offer.
 I guess i'm just not sure why you think menus and settings can be
tossed out the door ;)  Thanks for the vid though.
